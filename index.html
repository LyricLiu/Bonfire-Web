Wood Craft + Digital Intervention

Learn from the Inside

I began by directly engaging in wood practice. I found a wood funiture crafter in Atlanta and interviwed him. Then I designed a digital wood craft that stems from my desire to reimage how we might interact with ordinary furniture and how we could optimize user experience of wooden material.

Critical Reflection

My crafter responded to the question “Does any special property of wood inform and effect your practice?” by mentioning the natural beauty of wood. From his idea, I divided the natural beauty of wood into three facets:

Aesthetics

My crafter enjoys the hands-on manipulation of wood, through which he feels the surface of wood, and appreciates its various natural textures. As a natural resource, wood has special sensorial properties that provide rich aesthetic experience.

Sustainability

Sustainable design relates to people’s concerns about the environment. Because sustainability is a typical property of wood, Myers appreciates wood because this material is both renewable and clean and his furniture will last a long time.

Relatedness

Relatedness is an emotional response that people experience when they regularly make contact with an object. My crafter mentioned wood furniture inspires people to think about a simple and primitive lifestyle as well as an outdoor experience.

Digital Intervention

Based on these ideas, I created a hybrid craft that bridges digital practice and traditional wood craft. The outcome was an interactive piece of furniture called “Bonfire.” My intention was to create a dynamic process of experiencing the natural beauty of wood in the three ways mentioned above.

Interactive Bonfire

Bonfire is an interactive lamp that simulates a real-life outdoor bonfire in an indoor place. The input part is a pressure sensor that detects how many wood blocks are added in the box, the output part is a group of red LED lights. The brightness of the LED lights is controlled by the weight received from the pressure sensor.

Aesthetic

Users will touch the texture of the wood when they pick up a block and place it in the box. They will view the natural texture on the surface of the wood blocks and hear the sound of the blocks colliding with each other.

Sustainability

I collected wood remnants from Myer’s workshop and shaped them into appropriate blocks,In addition, the experience of interacting with natural wood blocks corresponded to the “go-back-to-nature” focus of sustainability.

Relatedness

When interacting with the lamp, people may access their memories of outdoor bonfires. The lamp brings outdoor experience into daily life by creating a peaceful and natural environment in an indoor space.

Reflection

Interactivity

This furniture is provocative. It opens up a dialogue to invite people to interact with it and control over it. The shaping of the lamp is based on the people’s interaction with the medium.Thus, interactors take ownership of this furniture and can shape it to their purposes and practices
